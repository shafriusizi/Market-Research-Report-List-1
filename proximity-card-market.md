<p><p>Proximity cards are categorized into three market types based on frequency: Low Frequency (LF), High Frequency (HF), and Ultra-High Frequency (UHF) cards. LF cards, operating at 125-134 kHz, are commonly used in applications like access control and animal tracking. HF cards, typically at 13.56 MHz, are popular in smart cards and contactless payments. UHF cards, working between 300 MHz and 3 GHz, are suitable for long-range applications such as supply chain management and vehicle tracking. Each type serves distinct functions in various industries.</p></p>
