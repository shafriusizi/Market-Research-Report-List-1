<p><p>The stomach cancer molecular diagnostics market includes various technologies for detecting genetic alterations associated with the disease. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) amplifies specific DNA sequences, facilitating mutation detection. Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH) visualizes chromosomal abnormalities using fluorescent probes. DNA sequencing provides detailed information about genetic mutations, crucial for targeted therapies. Gene chips, or microarrays, analyze multiple genes simultaneously to identify expression patterns linked to stomach cancer, aiding in diagnosis and treatment decisions. Together, these methods enhance precision in cancer management.</p></p>
