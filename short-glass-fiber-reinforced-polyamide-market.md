<p><p>The Short Glass Fiber Reinforced Polyamide market consists of two primary molding types: injection molding and extrusion molding. Injection molding is a process where molten polyamide mixed with short glass fibers is injected into a mold to create complex shapes, offering high precision and excellent surface finish. Conversely, extrusion molding involves forcing the polyamide mixture through a shaped die to produce continuous profiles or sheets. Both methods enhance the mechanical properties and thermal stability of polyamide, making it suitable for various applications in automotive, electronics, and consumer goods.</p></p>
